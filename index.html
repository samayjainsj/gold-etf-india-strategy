🧠 How to Pick the Right Gold ETF

1. Expense Ratio

Even a 0.3% difference compounds heavily over 10+ years. Prefer ratios under 0.5%.

2. Tracking Error

How closely the ETF mirrors actual gold prices. Lower = better. Check factsheets.

3. Liquidity / Volumes

Higher daily traded volume → tighter bid-ask spread → cheaper to buy/sell.

🏁 Bottom-Line Takeaways

  1. For most investors: GOLDBEES, SBI Gold ETF, and HDFC Gold ETF strike the best balance of cost + liquidity.
  2. For cost-sensitive long-term investors: Mirae Asset, DSP, and Edelweiss Gold ETFs offer some of the lowest expense ratios.
  3. For lump-sum / large investors: Stick with high-AUM, high-volume ETFs to minimise impact cost.
  4. Tax note: Post Budget 2024, Gold ETFs held > 12 months qualify as long-term, taxed at 12.5% (without indexation).
  5. Diversify: Don't concentrate; consider blending Gold ETFs with Gold Funds-of-Funds or other asset classes.
